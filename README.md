
PRACTICAL-2
Definition- The practical involves preparing,transforming,and visualizing sales data from a super market scenario in Power Bi. The aim is to integrate,cleanse , and analyze details to get meaningful insights.

Outcomes/learning- Developed skills in power bi for appending multiple tables,cleaning and transforming columns,and applying data preparation techniques in power query.practiced creating  calculated fields, rounding values,and building interactive visualization .  
Required Tools-Power Bi desktop(Power query editor and Report view) .

Working- Appended sales data from two tables, performed various data cleaning steps( such as removing blanks,remaining columns and built bar chart and other visualization in Power Bi.  The report explores sales performance across branches and cities.   
 
Dataset Review
  
The dataset consists of Supermarket sales transactions containing details such as invoice id,city,branch,customer type,gender,product line,and unit price
STEP-1: Importing the supermarket data directly in Power Bi
Imported Supermarket data directly into power bi using the “Get Data” option.
          
STEP-2;Performing Transformation of dataset
it Used Power Bi query editor to transform the data i.e. , Removed columns,removed duplicate columns,renamed columns,, extracted year and renamed it ,again removed the duplicate column and extracted month and day name and again renamed.
                     
STEP-3: Added a custom column called “Price before Tax”
Added a custom called called ‘price before tax” that is equal to “ Quantity”* “Unit Price”. And after this we removed the “ cost of goods sold column”.
            

STEP-4: Rounding off Numerical values
Rounded off numeric columns  to two decimal places for consistency and ease of reporting.

STEP-5: Performing Visualization in Report view
Created a Power Bi dashboard to analyze supermarket sales ,featuring visuals such as:

1-A table summarizing the total grass income for each city, enabling branch-wise revenue comparison.
2-A bar chart displaying the average of total sales by branch name , highlighting sales performances across different location . 
3- A horizontal bar chart showing the number of customer order by product line , with segmentation by customer type(member or normal), providing insights into product popularity and customer preferences.
